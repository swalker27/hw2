Sophie Walker: Travel History

My Youth, 1987-2006

I was fortunate enough to be born in London and spent my early years traveling around Europe. We moved to San Diego when I was 7 and my parents used Thanksgiving breaks for us to see the US.

  • France: I was in love with France ever since I learned French in high school. The first time I saw Paris it was everything I hoped.
  • Germany:We loved the German Christmas markets.
  • United Kingdom: A lot of time spent with family and friends in London and Inverness.
  • United States: We had a particularly memorable train trip up the West Coast from Los Angeles to Seattle.
  • Mexico: I remember a favorite family trip to Rosarito.

College, 2006-2009

I took advantage of going to college in the UK by traveling all around Europe by train and by plane.

  • France: Lucky that Paris is such a short train ride from London.
  • Germany: My best friend from college noew lives in Munich so I have plenty more excuses to visit.
  • Denmark, Sweden: Took a beautiful train trip across the causeway from Copenhagen to Malmo.
  • Italy: A memorable two weeks of backpacking through Italy with a friend from college went all the way from Naples up to Milan.
  • Austria: Backpacked with my brother to Vienna and Salzburg.
  • Hungary: A short trip to visit a friend in Budapest including a visit to the baths.

After College, 2009-present

After college I wanted to expand my horizons beyond Europe and the US. I started with a trip to Nepal and haven't looked back.

  • Nepal, India: One of my favorite trips doing a Habitat for Humanity build in Pokhara, Nepal. We took a weekend hike in the Annapurnas and I had a chance to spend a couple of days in Kathmandu and 24 hours in Delhi. It was a once-in-a-lifetime trip!
  • Kenya, South Africa: Two work trips were my first chance to see Africa. Safari in the Maasai Mara was a highlight.
  • Hong Kong, Shanghai: A visit to see my brother studying abroad in Hong Kong.
  • Argentina, Uruguay, Brazil: I cannot recommend Rio enough - a city with an amazing geography!
  • Canada: It took me until my working life began to make it across to our northern neighbor.
